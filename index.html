To My Aadya

Words from the heart

From the moment we met, I knew there was something different about you. It wasn't just your smile or the way your eyes light up when you talk about things you love—it was the feeling that, somehow, I'd found home in a person.

Every conversation we share, every laugh, every comfortable silence—they all add up to something I never want to lose. You've shown me what it means to be truly seen, to be understood without having to explain myself. With you, I don't have to pretend. I can just be.

I love the way you polish my nails without me asking, how we can spend hours doing nothing and everything at once, and how even the most mundane moments feel special when you're beside me. You've turned ordinary days into memories I'll treasure forever.

There are moments when I look at you and I'm overwhelmed by how much I care. It's not just the big romantic gestures—it's the quiet domestic love, the ramen on the balcony, the "study" sessions that turn into just being together. It's you making me laugh until my sides hurt, and me never wanting the moment to end.

This past year has been a journey of growth, laughter, and falling deeper in love with you. From winter drives where we talked about everything and nothing, to that summer day in Lucknow when seeing your face at the metro station felt like coming home—every moment with you has shaped us into who we are today.

I don't want just moments anymore, Aadya. I want mornings where I wake up to you, routines we build together, comfortable silences, and a lifetime of growth. I want all the ordinary days and extraordinary nights. I want the life we're creating, one beautiful moment at a time.

Thank you for being you—for your kindness, your laughter, your patience, and your love. Thank you for choosing me every day, and for making me want to be better for you, for us, for the future we're building together.

This Valentine's Day and every day after, I choose you. I choose us. I choose this beautiful life we're creating together.

Forever yours,
Anshuman
